Connect with a survivor trained by SHARE to help you understand your health situation, deal with every step of the process, from diagnosis to survivorship, think through your choices, and find hope.
It's easy to get started. You can either:

1) Send an email to support@talkabouthealth.com and we'll match you with a trained survivor.
2) Send a direct message to one of the peer survivors below.
3) Call 866-891-2392 if you would like to speak directly with someone who's been there.

Trained peers will also be participating on TalkAboutHealth by answering questions and sharing thoughts. Feel free to reach out to them at any time.

SHARE Trained Survivors
Melissa
I was diagnosed in 2005 with estrogen positive, Stage 2 breast cancer. My treatment included lumpectomy, chemotherapy (ACT), and radiation. In 2010, I became a volunteer at SHARE, an organization in NYC that supports women with breast and ovarian cancer. I volunteer on its Hotline and facilitate an ongoing support group for women with breast cancer. I am in my fifties and I have two grown sons.



Viva
When I was diagnosed with breast cancer in 2005, I had every imaginable kind of support: a top-notch medical team, good health insurance, a loving mate, helpful kids, great friends, colleagues who covered for me when I needed them to. And still I struggled with fear and sadness. The things that helped me cope with those: sister survivors--and yoga.
