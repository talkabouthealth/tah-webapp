General Guidelines

We encourage you to:
- Listen and support others while engaging in fruitful, intelligent, enlightening, entertaining, educational, and considerate discussions.
- Be polite and thank users that help you.
- Answer questions and post thoughts to share experiences, knowledge, insights, and resources you feel would be helpful.
- Vote answers up or down based on how helpful, accurate, and complete they are.
- Suggest edits to questions and answers to improve the quality of information.
- Help to organize questions and topics to improve the content structure and make information easier to find.
- Complete your personal and health profile so we can effectively match you with other members and questions relevant to you.
- Review and customize your privacy settings to suit your needs.
- Mark answers "Not Helpful" when they do not answer the question or are misleading or inaccurate.
- Flag users, questions, answers, and any communications that do not follow community guidelines as well as duplicate questions.
- Flag content that is not up to date with current research and standards of care.

 Please do not:
- Ask others to give personal medical advice or make decisions for you.
- Give personal medical advice or tell others what to do.
- Cause other users to experience negative feelings.
- Make inappropriate comments. This includes impolite, unsympathetic, insensitive, derogatory, and discriminatory remarks that could target a philosophy, religion, gender, ethnicity, age, disability, or sexual preference.
- Post profanity or anything sexually explicit.
- Engage in personal attacks.
- Spam, advertise, or promote commercial entities.
- Try to obtain personal information, that could include names, addresses, and email addresses.
- Post your own or others’ personal information, including email addresses or other contact information.
- Promote or endorse graphic or gratuitous violence, illegal activity, or any action designed to inflict intentional harm on anyone.
- Do anything illegal.


Question Guidelines

Questions should ...
- be clear and precise providing context and background information when appropriate.
- use standard english and complete sentences with correct spelling, grammar, and punctuation to make content accessible and understandable for everyone.
- be general, not specific to a particular user.
- have a constructive, fair, and impartial tone.
- seek narrative answers and are sufficiently open ended.
- inspire peers to share experiences rather than asking for opinions.
- insist that opinion be backed up with facts and references.
- invite answers that explain “why” and “how”.

 Questions should not ...
- request answers that are all equally valid (i.e. "What's your favorite X?"). Ask why or give the reason for your question.
- be answers, statements, or opinions. Use “Thoughts” for these.
- be duplicated. Before asking a question, make sure it has not already been asked.

Answer Guidelines

 Answers should ...
- be based on personal experiences or research that can be backed up with a reference. Share what you have done, rather than what you think. "I heard it somewhere" is not valid.
- be courteous, respectful, and open minded, there is usually no "right" answer. There can be opposing theories and strategies that are appropriate for different people. It is okay to disagree as long as both sides are providing their own facts (references to other sources and personal experiences).
- directly respond to the question.
- be honest and authentic while presenting a point of view accurately and in context. Not presenting any point of view as "the truth" or "the best view".
- cite verifiable, authoritative sources, especially on controversial topics.

Answers should not ...
- tell someone what to do or recommend personal medical advice.
- be inaccurate, unhelpful, or misleading.
- be based completely on an opinion.
- be excessively self-promoting. If you happen to have written a blog post that directly answers a question, it's okay to link to that, but please post a summary in your answer.
- promote a product or business if you're directly involved in it, unless someone asked a question specifically about it.
- Astroturfing is specifically prohibited.


 Topic Guidelines

 - Accurate and directly relevant topics should be associated with a question. This includes relevant to the question details, broad parent topics, more specific topics, and topics that are relevant to the subject matter of the question but not explicitly mentioned.
- Currently topics may be created and questions may be assigned to topics by anyone in the community with the intention of improving organizational structure. In the future, a reputation system will be developed to provide more structure to who may organize content.
- Topic names should be capitalized the same way a title would. Always capitalize the first and last word of a topic name, and capitalize all other words except for articles, conjunctions, and prepositions less than five letters.
- Nouns are preferred as topic names, rather than verbs, adjectives or questions.
- The plural form of the noun should normally be used.
- If there is controversy over a topic's name, Wikipedia's conventions can usually be used as an example.


 Reviewer Guidelines

 What do reviewers do?
Reviewers ensure that all content on TalkAboutHealth is acceptable according to the community guidelines, as well as up to date with current research and standards of care.

Who are the reviewers?
Right now, we have a several appointed volunteers reviewing. In the future we will have also have a reputation system that will enable experienced members of the community to review content and interactions.

What are the responsibilities of a reviewers?
The reviewer’s goal is to guide and set an example for the community. Reviewers should be compassionate, but firm and always respectful, transparent, and fair. Reviewers actions represent the community, so they will be held to a high standard of behavior.

Specific responsibilities include:
1. Edit questions, question details, topic names, and topic details to make them more accessible and understandable.
2. Organize topics and questions to make content easier to find.
3. Suggest edits to answers to improve quality of content.
4. Mark answers as “Not helpful” for content that should be collapsed.
5. Flag content that should be removed from the site, duplicate questions, or that is not up to date with current research or standards of care.
6. Explain the reasoning for all actions taken on posts. This is important so that members can learn the norms of the community and review policies.


 Administrator Guidelines

 What does an administrator do?
Administrators do as little as possible. But sometimes an administrator is needed to ensure content and user interactions meet community guidelines.

Who are adminstrators?
Right now, several representatives from TalkAboutHealth are administrators.

What are the responsibilities of a moderator?
The administrator’s goal is to guide and set an example for the community. Adminstrators should be compassionate, but firm and always respectful, transparent, and fair. Administrator actions represent the community, so they will be held to a high standard of behavior. Specific responsibilities include:

1. Check all flagged posts, and decide if further action is needed.
2. Merge duplicate questions and topics.
3. Remove answers and questions that do not follow community guidelines or that are off topic.
4. Ban users who do not follow community guidelines.
5. Explain the reasoning for all actions taken on posts. This is important so that members can learn the norms of the community and the moderation policies.
6. Communicate directly with users via email to help mediate and resolve disputes.


 Reference Guidelines

 - Acceptable references are third-party, published sources with a reputation for fact-checking and accuracy.
- Source material must have been published (made public in some form).
- Sources should directly support the material presented in an answer and be appropriate to the claims made.
- Acceptable publication include academic and peer-reviewed publications, mainstream publications, university-level textbooks, books published by respected publishing houses, magazines, journals, and mainstream newspapers.


TalkAboutHealth will review all flags and suspend accounts if the above guidelines are not followed or if any other behavior is deemed to be inappropriate or unacceptable.

TalkAboutHealth reserves the right to change the Community Guidelines as new situations come to our attention and we find it necessary to alter or expand upon the policies. We will notify the community of the changes as promptly as possible.
